Chess Titles

  • World Chess Champion

    Youngest: Gukesh Dommaraju became the youngest undisputed World Chess Champion at 18 years and 195 days in December 2024, defeating Ding Liren.

    Oldest: Wilhelm Steinitz (Austria) was the oldest to win the World Chess Championship at 50 years old in 1886, becoming the first official World Champion.

  • Grand Master (GM)

    In order to achieve this title, a player must reach an established classical or standard FIDE rating of 2500 and earn three grandmaster norms in international competition.

    Youngest: Abhimanyu Mishra at 12 years, 4 months, and 25 days

    Oldest: Yuri Shabanov at 66 years old

  • International Master (IM)

    To earn this title, a player must reach an established classical or standard FIDE rating of 2400 and achieve three international master norms in international competition.

    Youngest: Faustino Oro (Argentina) at age 10 in 2024.

    Oldest: George Thomas (United Kingdom) was awarded the International Master title at the age of 69 in 1950.

  • FIDE Master (FM)

    The FIDE Master title is earned by any player who achieves a classical or standard FIDE rating of 2300 in international competition. No norms are required.

    Youngest: Alekhine Nouri (Philippines) at age 7

  • Candidate Master (CM)

    The Candidate Master title is earned by any player who achieves a classical or standard FIDE rating of 2200 in international competition. No norms are required.

    Youngest: Ethan Pang (UK) at age 9

    Oldest: Henry Camilleri (Malta) was awarded the Candidate Master title in 2011 at the age of 78.

  • World Chess Champion

    Youngest: Hou Yifan (China) became the youngest Women's World Chess Champion at 16 years and 300 days in December 2010.

    Oldest: Lyudmila Rudenko (Soviet Union) became Women's World Chess Champion at 46 years old in 1950.

  • Woman Grandmaster (WGM)

    This title is exclusive to women and is earned by achieving a classical or standard FIDE rating of 2300 as well as three WGM norms in international competition.

    Youngest: Hou Yifan (China) at age 14.

    Oldest: Lyudmila Rudenko (Soviet Union) was awarded the Woman Grandmaster title in 1976 at the age of 72.

  • Woman International Master (WIM)

    This title is exclusive to women and is earned by achieving a classical or standard FIDE rating of 2200. Three norms are required.

    Youngest: Nurgyul Salimova (Bulgaria) at age 8.

  • Woman FIDE Master (WFM)

    This title is exclusive to women and is earned by achieving a classical or standard FIDE rating of 2100. No norms are required.

    Oldest: Ingrīda Priedīte (Latvia) was awarded the Woman FIDE Master title in 2014 at the age of 60.

  • Woman Candidate Master (WCM)

    This title is exclusive to women and is earned by achieving a classical or standard FIDE rating of 2000. No norms are required.
